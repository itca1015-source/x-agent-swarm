# X Article Posting Rules

Use these defaults whenever preparing or posting a new X Article from this folder.

1. Translate Chinese source drafts into English before publishing.
2. Include informative images, preferably diagrams or data cards that explain the article. Use at least three; for long deep dives, prefer around six.
3. Preserve paragraph boundaries: each `article.json` `p` block should paste into X as its own Draft.js paragraph block.
4. Do not merge separate paragraphs with soft line breaks inside one X paragraph; published text then reads as multiple blocks jammed into the same paragraph.
5. Do not insert extra empty paragraph blocks between normal paragraphs; use one real paragraph block per source paragraph.
6. Use the generated page's "Copy Article Text" button when possible; it should preserve one X paragraph per `p` block.
7. If autoposting directly into X, verify spacing in Preview before publishing. Images can remain separate media blocks at their intended positions.
7a. Never publish visible image placeholders. Before final publish, check that the body contains no `[ IMAGE ... ]` markers and no failed camera placeholder glyphs. If images cannot be inserted cleanly, remove the image slots and publish clean text only.
7b. For X Articles in the in-app browser, do not rely on embedded HTML image paste. Paste generated PNGs as binary image clipboard items into the editor, then verify the live post has real `pbs.twimg.com/media` images before calling it done.
8. Keep a human review step before final publish, especially after editing a live article.
8a. When pasting or posting on X, verify the active account is `hunterguo101` before entering content.
9. Images should carry explanatory load, not just decoration. Put key frameworks, comparisons, quotes, or summaries directly in the images.
10. Avoid a stiff corporate-deck look. Prefer attractive editorial cards, warm backgrounds, clear hierarchy, and readable text.
11. Spread images throughout the article to reduce wall-of-text fatigue.
12. Put the strongest idea in the first 3-5 paragraphs. Do not hide the real hook behind background setup.
13. Open concrete, not abstract: use a number, a pain point, a surprising detail, a familiar tool, or a small scene before the thesis.
14. Write for someone taking a short break from work: short paragraphs, one idea per paragraph, conversational transitions, low friction.
15. Avoid AI-sounding essay language such as "this is a signal", "it is worth noting", "this demonstrates", and long thesis setup.
16. Titles should be curiosity-first and low-jargon. They should attract readers who do not already care about the specific models or companies.
17. The companion X thread should state the big idea directly, not merely tease the article.
18. Keep caveats, but make them crisp and human rather than formal or defensive.
