# BlockRun (@BlockRunAI) X Playbook — Reverse-Engineered (COLD-START focus)

**Basis:** 637 scraped records (06-01-2026), spanning **2025-12-25 → 2026-06-01** — i.e. the account's *entire life from day one*. Joined **December 2025**; ~676 total posts. 408 authored by @BlockRunAI + 229 reposts. Engagement = exact likes/reposts/replies + views.

> **Why this account matters:** Unlike OpenRouter (a scaled leader) and Nansen (355K followers), BlockRun is a **brand-new, tiny account we caught from post #1**. Both Flatkey and BTCMind are early-stage, so BlockRun is the most directly transferable model: it shows *what the first six months actually look like* — including how long it stays small before anything works. See [[openrouter_playbook]] (format/destination template) and [[nansen_playbook]] (crypto scaled template).

> **What BlockRun is:** an **x402-based pay-per-request AI gateway on Base** — AI agents pay for LLM/API calls with USDC stablecoin micropayments, no API keys. So it's a hybrid: **a gateway like Flatkey** *and* **crypto-native like BTCMind**. That dual relevance is why it's worth studying for both products.

## 0. THE COLD-START STORY (the part to actually copy)

### Day 1 (Dec 25, 2025) — launch with a concrete spec, not a vision
Their first-ever post was the single best move:
- **21❤ 1,392👁** — "Just launched BlockRun using x402 V2. AI agents can pay for AI services with stablecoin. • 618+ x402 services in one place • GPT-5.2, Gemini 2.5 Pro, o1…" → a *specific, numeric* launch drop (same shape as OpenRouter's, just at 1% the scale).
- Same day, a reply: *"We are cooking… let's grow the community first."* — community-first framing from minute one.

### Week 1 — build in public, ship artifacts, post the proof
Days 2–7 were a stream of **concrete developer artifacts**, not marketing:
- "SDK dropping soon — `from blockrun_llm import LLMClient` … one line. That's it." (show the code)
- "Just open-sourced the BlockRun Python SDK … `pip install …`" (GitHub link)
- **The hero demo:** "Generate images in Claude Code with crypto micropayments… → Signs USDC on Base → Returns image $0.05/image" + "I asked Claude Code to visualize its thoughts. This is what it made." (20❤ — a *showable* demo tied to a hot tool).

### Weeks 2–4 — attach to a rising narrative + manual community building
- Latched onto the **x402 / Base / agentic-payments** narrative and hot dev tools (**Claude Code, OpenClaw**). They didn't invent demand — they surfed it.
- **Community Launch (Jan 4):** "Launching BlockRun Builders — a community for developers building with x402" (32❤, their early best) + a public **leaderboard** product + Telegram link.
- Replies were used to *recruit*: "What's your plan? DM you", "Just help with repost", asking ecosystem accounts directly.

### The honest timeline — it stayed SMALL for ~4 months
Median likes on originals by month: **Dec 8 · Jan 7 · Feb 6 · Mar 6 · Apr 11**. Four months of single-digit medians. Then the breakout:
- **Apr 22 (original): 342❤ / 1.38M👁** — "LLM Router for Your OpenClaw to save your Token"
- **Apr 25 (reply): 972❤ / 5.7M👁** — same hook restated.
- **Apr 10: 287❤ / 1.17M👁** — "Agent Native LLM Router to save your Token, Pay as you go."

**The lesson is the single most important one for Flatkey/BTCMind:** the breakout was *not* a new feature — it was finally **nailing a narrow, benefit-led hook** ("**LLM Router for [hot tool OpenClaw] to save your token**") and attaching it to a tool that was itself going viral. Everything before April was groundwork that looked like it wasn't working. Don't quit at month 3.

## 6. Honest caveats / where BlockRun is a *weaker* model than OpenRouter
- **Tiny sample of winners.** Almost all engagement comes from ~5 April posts; the median post got 1–8 likes. The "playbook" is really 3–4 breakout posts + a lot of grind. Treat the breakout hooks as hypotheses, not proven formats.
- **Different product shape.** BlockRun is crypto-payments-native (x402/Base/USDC). Its narrative tailwind (x402 hype) is borrowed reach Flatkey won't have — Flatkey must find its *own* rising tide (model launches, agent tools, cost pain). For **BTCMind**, the crypto-narrative attachment transfers more directly.
- **It worked partly because of timing** — x402 was a hot 2026 narrative. The transferable lesson is "find and attach to your category's hot narrative," not "use x402."

## 7. Recommendation: how to use BlockRun vs OpenRouter
- **OpenRouter = the destination & format library** (what mature, winning content in this category looks like).
- **BlockRun = the cold-start sequencing** (what to actually do in months 0–6 with no audience): launch with a spec, build in public, attach to a rising narrative + hot tools, borrow reach via reposts, grind replies/community, publish milestones, and iterate hooks until one breaks out.
- **For the Poster agent specifically:** in the first months, down-weight "broadcast originals" and up-weight (a) build-in-public artifact drops, (b) milestone posts, (c) narrow benefit-hook launch drops attached to whatever tool/model is trending that week. Pair it heavily with a Reposter (amplify narrative leaders) and a Replier (show up in builder threads) — because at zero followers those out-perform originals, exactly as they did for BlockRun.
